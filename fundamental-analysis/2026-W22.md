## Per-Currency Detail (Non-Neutral)

### 🟢🟢 SGD — Strong Bullish
MAS tightened policy in April 2026 by steepening the S$NEER slope — a direct hawkish signal. GDP grew +6% YoY / +4% annualised. Services PMI hit 57.9, retail sales surged +3.7% MoM, and the trade surplus stands at SGD 13B. Unemployment is a healthy 2.1%. Inflation at 1.8% gives MAS room without overheating. Across nearly every dimension, Singapore is the strongest macro picture in the 19-currency basket this week.

### 🟢 JPY — Mild Bullish
BoJ is on the verge of its next rate hike — ING flags June, BofA flags June/October. Spring wage negotiations produced +5.09% average increase (third consecutive year above 5%). Real wages are +1.9% (nominal 3.3% vs CPI 1.4%) — a genuine purchasing-power improvement. Manufacturing PMI 54.5 and retail +1.3% MoM support domestic demand. Zero bearish indicators. Below-target CPI is an anomaly given the wage data — the BoJ expects it to rise.

### 🟢 AUD — Mild Bullish
RBA hiked for the third time in 2026 (to 4.35%). CPI at 4.2% is above the 2–3% band and core inflation at 3.4% is sticky — the RBA has no room to pause. GDP at 3.2% annualised (Dec/25 — update pending) and unemployment at 4.5% provide cover for continued hiking. Risk: services PMI at 47.7 is contracting, the trade balance swung sharply to -A$1.8B deficit (from +A$5B surplus), and real wages are -0.9% — the consumer may be squeezed. Net: bullish from the rate cycle; monitor services and trade deterioration.

### 🟢 NOK — Mild Bullish
Norges Bank held at 4.25% but explicitly revised the rate path upward. The committee flagged it is "likely appropriate to raise the policy rate at one of forthcoming meetings," pointing to 4.25–4.50% by year-end. CPI at 3.4% remains above the 2% target. The trade surplus is massive at NOK 84B, underpinned by oil exports. Norway PMI data discontinued Dec 2025 — data gap noted. Consumer confidence is weak (-20 points) — key risk if oil prices fall.

### 🔴 EUR — Mild Bearish
The ECB hike expected in June (2.15% → 2.25%) provides a floor, but the underlying economy is weakening. Services PMI at 46.4 is in clear contraction — the largest sector of the eurozone economy. Retail sales are -0.1% MoM. Real wages are negative (-0.54%: negotiated wages 2.46% vs CPI 3.0%). Unemployment at 6.2% exceeds the G10 healthy threshold. GDP expansion is 0.1% QoQ / 0.4% annualised — near-stall. The ECB is hiking into weakness — a stagflationary dynamic — which historically does not sustain currency strength. 3 bullish signals (inflation, rate hike, trade surplus) are outweighed by 4 bearish ones.

### 🔴 CAD — Mild Bearish
Canada's macro picture has deteriorated materially. Unemployment climbed to 6.9%. GDP is flat at 0.0% QoQ / -0.1% YoY (borderline recession). Consumer confidence collapsed to 45.6. Employment fell 17,700 in April with full-time positions -46,700. The BoC is holding at 2.25% with cut risk elevated. Government debt/GDP at 114%. The only positives are mfg PMI 53.3 and retail +0.6% — inconsistent with the broader picture. US tariff impacts are a structural headwind.

### 🔴 SEK — Mild Bearish
Sweden is in deflation (CPI -0.1%) with the Riksbank firmly on hold at 1.75% (easing cycle complete, no more cuts). GDP contracted -0.2% QoQ (-0.8% annualised). Unemployment is 8.7% — above the SEK-adjusted neutral band (7–8.5%). The trade balance turned negative in April (SEK -7.3B). The outlier is PMI: manufacturing 57.2 and services 52.5 are in solid expansion — may point to future improvement but hasn't translated to growth or inflation yet. Score the data.

### 🔴 ILS — Mild Bearish
GDP contracted -0.8% QoQ (-3.2% annualised). Retail sales -1.7% MoM, trade deficit widened to -$4.2B, consumer confidence at -20.4 (very weak). The Middle East conflict is the dominant driver — the economy is under structural strain. BoI holding at 3.75%. Only green: unemployment 2.9% (tight). **Avoid as trade candidate** — geopolitical tail risk, low cross-pair liquidity.
