## Per-Currency Commentary

**AUD** — The standout performer this week. The RBA raised the cash rate to 4.35% in May 2026, marking its third consecutive hike. CPI at 4.2% is well above the 2–3% target band, and the Board projects underlying inflation above 3% until late 2027. GDP is growing at +3.2% annualised. The hiking cycle is the clearest fundamental driver in the G8 right now. The primary risk: services PMI at 47.7 (contracting) and a trade deficit — if domestic demand weakens while the RBA hikes, stagflation risk emerges.

**JPY** — Upgraded from W21. The BOJ held at 0.75% in April but the vote was 6-3 with dissenters wanting an immediate hike. Markets price +50bps through 2026, with Bank of America calling a June hike. Manufacturing PMI strong at 54.5, trade surplus intact, and the carry trade unwind thesis is building. Main risk: Middle East conflict uncertainty may delay hikes.

**EUR** — Improved from W21's Strong Bearish but still the weakest major. Services PMI at 46.4 (firmly in contraction) is the key negative. Unemployment at 6.2% leaves slack. ECB held in April but has shifted from cutting to hold — that is a positive change, but markets pricing potential hikes in 2026 seems premature given GDP at +0.4% annualised. Energy inflation (10.8% YoY) is the wildcard — could force the ECB to hike despite weak growth.

**ILS** — Clearest short in the basket. GDP contracted -0.8% QoQ, retail sales fell -1.7%, trade deficit widened, and the Bank of Israel continues cutting to support a war-impacted economy. Geopolitical risk is elevated and ongoing. Note: USD/ILS is an NDF market — check spreads before trading.

**USD** — Steady. Fed held at 3.75% with an 8-4 split vote. Inflation remains sticky at 3.8%, making cuts unlikely in 2026. Manufacturing PMI strong at 55.3. The 47% probability of a hike by July 2027 suggests the next move is more likely up than down. Trade deficit at -$60B is the structural negative.

**NOK** — Upgraded. Norges Bank is ready to hike — rate path forecasts a 25bp hike in Q3, possibly another in Q4. CPI at 3.4% above the 2% target, core CPI 3.2%. Large oil-linked surplus (NOK 84B). Main risk: GDP contracted -0.3% QoQ (Dec/25 data — may be stale) and retail fell -0.1%.

**CAD** — Conflicted. GDP contracted -0.2% QoQ and unemployment rose to 6.9%. BoC cutting cycle ongoing at 2.25%. However, manufacturing PMI improved to 53.3 and retail +0.6%. The negative macro indicators dominate — mild bearish lean despite some positives.

**CHF** — Unchanged from W21 in bias but with a rate discrepancy flagged. CPI 0.6% (within 0-2% SNB band), SNB on hold at 0% with no hike until H2 2027. Strong PMIs (mfg 54.5, svcs 54.8) and surplus support CHF structurally, but the rate differential vs hiking central banks works against it.
