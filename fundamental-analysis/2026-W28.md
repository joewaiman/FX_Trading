## Per-Currency Detail вҖ” Material Changes Only

### в¬ҶпёҸ JPY вҖ” Conflicted вҶ’ Mild Bullish
The BoJ's June hike to 1.00% took the policy rate to a 31-year high, and board member Tamura has guided to 25bp increments "at intervals of a few months" toward a ~2% neutral rate, with economists penciling 1.25% by year-end. The fundamental picture has caught up with the policy shift: **real wages are now positive at +1.7%** (labour cash earnings 3.2% vs CPI 1.5%), retail sales rose 1.9% MoM, and both PMIs are comfortably expansionary (mfg 54.8, services 52.2). The two remaining drags вҖ” CPI still below the 2% target and a ВҘ379B trade deficit вҖ” are the same ones that have persisted for a decade. The difference now is a central bank actively removing accommodation while every peer pauses. JPY is no longer a funding currency by default.

### в¬ҶпёҸ NZD вҖ” Conflicted вҶ’ Mild Bullish (new catalyst)
On **8 July** the RBNZ raised the OCR 25bp to 2.50% вҖ” its first hike in three years, decided unanimously (versus May's 3вҖ“3 deadlock broken by the Governor's casting vote). The statement said further hikes "appear likely at upcoming meetings"; analysts expect ~3.00% by year-end via September and December moves. CPI at 3.1% sits above the 1вҖ“3% band's upper bound. Manufacturing PMI surged to **59.7** from 49.9 in W26 вҖ” the sharpest improvement anywhere in the basket. The blemish is services PMI at 47.5 (contraction) and unemployment at 5.3%. W26 rated NZD Conflicted on the grounds that the "cutting cycle is complete but no new catalyst." **The catalyst has now arrived.**

### в¬ҮпёҸ AUD вҖ” Mild Bullish вҶ’ Conflicted
Two of W26's three bullish pillars have gone. The trade balance flipped from a **+A$1.8B surplus to a вҖ“A$3.0B deficit**, and newly available wage data shows the Wage Price Index at 3.3% against CPI of 4.0% вҖ” **real wages negative at вҖ“0.7%**. The RBA held at 4.35% in July and three of the four major Australian banks now believe the cash rate has peaked, with cuts penciled for 2027. CPI above the 2вҖ“3% band and unemployment at 4.4% remain supportive, but the momentum has turned. AUD no longer passes the entry gate as a long leg.

### в¬ҶпёҸ SEK вҖ” Mild Bearish вҶ’ Conflicted *(see headline finding)*
