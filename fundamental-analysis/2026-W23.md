## Per-Currency Narrative (Non-Neutral)

**SGD — Strong Bullish**
The MAS steepened the S$NEER slope in April 2026, its tightest policy stance in over a year, citing imported inflation from the Middle East conflict. GDP growth is running at 6.0% YoY — the strongest in the G19 basket. Services composite PMI at 56.7 and retail sales up 3.7% MoM confirm broad domestic strength. The trade surplus of SGD 13B is the second-largest in the basket (after CNY). SGD is the strongest fundamental long candidate this week.

**JPY — Mild Bullish**
The BoJ remains committed to its gradual hiking cycle, with the next move expected around October 2026, taking rates toward 1.0%. The Shunto spring wage round delivered ~5% wage growth — the largest in decades — supporting domestic consumption (retail +1.3% MoM) and giving the BoJ justification to continue hiking. Manufacturing PMI at 54.5 is strong. The only concern is CPI at 1.4% (below the 2.0% target), which could slow the pace of hikes. Trade surplus confirmed.

**NOK — Mild Bullish**
Norges Bank has signalled a rate hike from the current 4.25% to 4.25–4.50% before year-end. CPI at 3.4% remains above target. Norway's trade surplus is NOK 84B — enormous, underpinned by oil exports. The Middle East conflict has pushed energy prices higher, benefiting Norway's terms of trade. Manufacturing production +3.2% YoY. Main risk: oil price volatility.

**GBP — Mild Bearish**
The UK consumer is under clear pressure — retail sales fell 1.3% MoM in April, services PMI contracted to 49.3, and the trade deficit widened to −£9.7B. GDP is growing modestly (0.6% QoQ) but BoE Governor Bailey has explicitly ruled out urgency to hike. CPI at 2.8% is above target but moderating. The combination of weak consumer data, services contraction, and a cautious central bank makes GBP a reasonable short candidate against stronger currencies.

**SEK — Mild Bearish**
Sweden's CPIF fell to 0.8% — well below the 2.0% Riksbank target — and GDP contracted 0.2% QoQ in Q1. The Riksbank has held at 1.75% for five consecutive meetings with a dovish tilt. Trade balance is in deficit. The two bright spots (manufacturing PMI 57.3, services 53.9) are strong but insufficient to offset the deflation risk and negative GDP. Riksbank cut is more likely than a hike from current levels.

**CAD — Mild Bearish**
Canada's labour market continues to deteriorate — unemployment hit 6.9% in April, rising from 6.7% the prior month. GDP was flat QoQ and slightly negative YoY (−0.1%). The BoC held at 2.25% in April but explicitly noted both a hike and cut remain possible depending on energy/trade dynamics. Manufacturing PMI (52.9) and a trade surplus swing are positives, but the growth and employment picture is bearish. BoC decision June 10 is the immediate risk event.
