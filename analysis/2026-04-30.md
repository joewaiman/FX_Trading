## Per-Currency Detail

### AUD — Strong Bull (7/8)

The RBA has already delivered two consecutive rate hikes (February and March), and the May 5 decision is now widely expected to deliver a third. Governor Bullock confirmed the board agrees rates may need to rise further. The only blemishes are marginal PMI readings (Mfg 51, Svcs 50.3) and unavailable March retail sales. AUD is the clearest long in the basket.

### JPY — Strong Bull (7/9)

The Bank of Japan held at 0.75% in April but in a 6–3 split — the largest dissent under Governor Ueda. Three board members (Nakagawa, Takata, Tamura) voted for an immediate hike to 1.0%. The BoJ revised its core inflation forecast sharply higher to 2.8% (from 1.9%), and markets now price a 74% probability of a June hike. Manufacturing PMI is a standout at 54.9. The only drag is CPI at 1.5% YoY (currently below the 2% target), but the BoJ's own upgraded forecast overrides this — rate bias dominates. Unemployment at 2.7% flags potential overheating risk. Strong rate hike conviction makes JPY a compelling long.

### USD — Mild Bull (6/9)

CPI at 3.3% remains above target; retail sales were strong (+1.7%); manufacturing PMI at 54 is solid. However, GDP growth has slowed sharply (0.5% annualised), and the trade deficit at -$57B is a persistent headwind. The Fed's uncertain footing (political pressure on Powell, inflation stickiness) keeps USD as a mild long rather than a conviction trade.

CHF scores as mild bull on the strength of its real economy indicators and haven status, but without a rate catalyst it is not a conviction long.

The rate hike signal earns EUR a mild bullish score, but the conviction gap vs AUD or JPY is wide. EUR is best used as the short leg against a stronger currency, not as a standalone long.
