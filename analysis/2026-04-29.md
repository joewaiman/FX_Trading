## Per-Currency Detail

**AUD (Strong Bull — 8.0/9):** The RBA is the most hawkish major central bank, having raised the cash rate to 4.10% for the second consecutive meeting. CPI at 3.7% is above the 2–3% target band, unemployment at 4.3% is within the optimal range, and the Iran oil shock adds further upside inflation pressure. A third consecutive hike in May is widely expected. Trade surplus of AUD 5,686M reflects commodity export strength.

**GBP (Strong Bull — 7.0/9):** Supported by elevated inflation (3.3% vs 2% target), strong PMI readings in manufacturing (53.6) and services (52.0), and solid retail sales (+0.7% MoM). BoE holds April 30 but one hike by December is fully priced, with inflation forecast to spike to ~3.5% due to Iran energy shock. Main weakness is near-zero GDP growth (0.1% QoQ).

**JPY (Tentative Bull — 6.0/9):** Key driver is BoJ rate normalisation. CPI at 1.5% is still below the 2% target but the BoJ revised its FY2026 inflation forecast to 2.8% and three board members voted for an immediate hike to 1.0%. Next hike expected July 2026. Manufacturing PMI at 54.9 is the strongest in the basket. Headwinds: retail sales -2.0% MoM and the Iran growth shock.

**EUR (HOLD — 5.0/9):** Conflicted. Inflation above target (2.6%) and manufacturing PMI tipping into expansion (52.2) are positives, but services PMI is in contraction (47.4), retail sales fell -0.2%, and unemployment is high at 6.2%. ECB faces genuine dilemma — hold April 30 with possible summer hike, but eurozone growth is stalling. Too many crosscurrents to trade directionally.

**CAD (Mild Bear — 3.0/9):** Weakest fundamental currency in the basket. GDP contracted in Q4 2025 (-0.6% QoQ annualised), unemployment at 6.7% is well above the 5.5% threshold, services PMI in contraction (47.2), and trade balance in deficit (-CAD 5,740M). BoC bias is dovish — rate cuts seen as more likely than hikes. Oil export revenues from Iran war are the only offset but have not translated into domestic economic strength.
