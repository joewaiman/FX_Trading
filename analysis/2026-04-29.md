## Per-Currency Detail

### AUD — 🟢 Mild Bullish (6/9)
AUD is the top-ranked currency this week. The RBA is expected to deliver a rate hike in May, with CPI running at 4.6% — well above the 2–3% target band. Labour market remains tight at 4.3% unemployment. GDP growth of +0.8% QoQ annualises to approximately 3.2% (above the 2% bullish threshold), and the trade account remains in surplus. The primary risk is domestic consumption data (retail missing this cycle); services PMI at 50.3 signals only marginal expansion. Fundamental bias firmly bullish into the May decision.

### NZD — 🟢 Mild Bullish (6/9)
NZD scores alongside AUD driven by RBNZ rate hike expectations and above-target inflation (3.1% vs 1–3% upper bound). Manufacturing PMI at 53.2 is solid, retail sales +0.9% and a trade surplus confirm broad-based support. The drag: unemployment at 5.4% is elevated and services PMI at 46.0 is in contraction — flagging uneven domestic conditions. The rate bias is the dominant signal.

### GBP — 🟢 Mild Bullish (5/9)
Sterling benefits from a hawkish BoE hold at 3.75% with upside rate risk — CPI at 3.3% remains well above the 2% target and the BoE has signalled no rush to cut. Manufacturing PMI at 53.6 confirms expansion. Retail +0.7% beats the bullish threshold. Weak points: GDP barely positive at +0.1% QoQ and unemployment at 4.9% (neutral range). GBP/EUR and GBP/CAD are the highest-conviction expressions.

### JPY — 🟢 Mild Bullish (5/9)
JPY scores bullish primarily on BoJ rate hike bias (ongoing normalisation from ultra-loose policy) and strong Mfg PMI at 54.9. Trade surplus +¥667B provides structural currency demand. The key contradiction: CPI at 1.5% is below the BoJ's 2% target, meaning the hike narrative is policy normalisation rather than inflation-fighting. Retail sales −2% MoM is a notable drag. JPY long positions best expressed versus EUR and CAD.

### USD — 🟡 Neutral (4/9)
The Fed is on hold at 3.75–4.0% with one cut priced into the forward curve, preventing a bullish rate bias score. Domestically the picture is mixed: ISM Manufacturing 54 and retail sales +1.7% are strong, but GDP +0.5% annualised is below-trend and the trade deficit at −$57.4B continues to widen. USD acts as the HOLD baseline — valid as the long leg vs CAD and EUR, but limited upside as a standalone BUY.

### CHF — 🟡 Neutral (4/9)
Switzerland presents a paradox: exceptional PMI readings (Mfg 53.3, Svcs 57.2 — best services number in the universe) and a trade surplus, yet the SNB is stuck at the zero lower bound with CPI at just 0.3%. The rate policy cannot be bullish when the economy is flirting with deflation. CHF remains a safe-haven demand story rather than a rate-driven BUY. Valid as long leg vs EUR and CAD.

### NOK — 🟡 HOLD / Bullish Lean (4/7, partial data)
Norges Bank is expected to hike to 4.25–4.5% from 4.0%, with CPI at 3.6% above the 2.5% target. The trade surplus is enormous (NOK97.5B — oil revenues). However GDP contracted −0.3% QoQ and retail −0.1% reflect domestic weakness. PMI data unavailable. If PMI confirms expansion, NOK would likely score 6+/9. SHORT EUR/NOK has medium conviction — flag as watchlist candidate pending PMI confirmation.

### SEK — 🟡 Neutral / Conflicted (4/9)
SEK is the most conflicted currency in the universe. Manufacturing PMI 56.3, Services PMI 55.7, retail sales +3.1% and a trade surplus are all strongly bullish. Yet unemployment at 9.7% is the worst in the universe, GDP is negative, and CPI at 0.6% is well below the 2% Riksbank target. HOLD — do not trade SEK based on PMIs alone without macro confirmation.

### EUR — 🔴 Mild Bearish (2/9)
EUR is the primary short target this cycle. The ECB has cut rates aggressively to 2.15% and is now on hold — neither cutting further nor hiking. Unemployment at 6.2% exceeds the bearish threshold. Services PMI at 47.4 is in contraction. Retail sales −0.2% MoM is negative. The only support: above-target CPI (2.6%) and Mfg PMI 52.2. EUR SHORT is the cleanest expression in the universe; best paired against AUD and NZD.

### CAD — 🔴 Mild Bearish (2/9)
CAD is the second-ranked short. The BoC cut from 5% to 2.25% (most aggressive easing cycle globally), GDP contracted −0.2% QoQ, unemployment at 6.7% is well above the 5.5% bearish threshold, and the trade deficit is widening (−CAD5.7B). US-Canada trade tensions add further downside risk. The only green flags: CPI 2.4% slightly above target and retail +0.6%. AUD/CAD, GBP/CAD, and NZD/CAD are all high-conviction setups.

### HKD — Sell / USD Proxy
HKD trades in a narrow band pegged to USD under the Linked Exchange Rate System. Rate policy mirrors the Fed exactly. No independent fundamental signal — this is effectively USD exposure with a structural trade deficit and collapsed retail (−6%). Not a standalone FX trading pair; excluded from active pair construction.

### ILS — Hold (insufficient data)
Cut cycle paused following geopolitical events. BoI at 4%. CPI 1.9% is within the 1–3% target band. GDP growth solid at +1.0% QoQ. Trade deficit −$3.1B is the main negative. PMI, retail sales, and services data unavailable. ILS/USD spot liquidity is limited. Flag for monitoring; avoid trading until full dataset available.
