## Per-Currency Detail

### GBP (7/9 — Strong Bull)
GBP is now the standout currency in the full basket. The BoE voted 8-1 to hold at 3.75% in April with one member already voting to hike. Q1 2026 GDP came in at 0.6% QoQ (strongest quarterly growth in the basket). Manufacturing PMI 53.7 and Services PMI 52.7 are both solidly in expansion. Retail sales +0.7% MoM beat expectations. CPI eased from 3.3% to 2.8% (April data) but remains 80bp above target — keeps hike risk alive into the June 18 MPC meeting. The only drag is the trade deficit. GBP is a clear buy against the field.

### EUR (5/9 — Mild Bull, upgraded)
The ECB story has inverted. After holding unanimously at 2.15% in April, rising energy inflation (CPI 3.0%, above 2% target) has pushed markets to price 3 hikes in 2026 — first expected at the June 5 meeting (78% probability). This is a complete reversal from the "ECB stuck" narrative of a week ago. Manufacturing PMI improved to 52.2. The drags remain significant: Services PMI 47.6 (contraction), retail -0.1%, unemployment 6.2%, GDP 0.1% QoQ. EUR is a mild buy on rate expectations, not on economic health. Buy EUR vs weaker currencies (PLN, CHF, NZD); avoid EUR shorts vs other bulls.

### JPY (5/9 — Mild Bull)
The BoJ June hike thesis is strengthening. The 6-3 April vote was the most divided under Ueda's governorship; board members stated a hike is "quite possible" from the next meeting. ING noted that stronger-than-expected Q1 GDP (0.5% QoQ) supports the June case. Mfg PMI 55.1 remains the strongest in the basket. CPI at 1.5% is below the 2% target but the BoJ is forward-looking — when the central bank is hiking, the currency is a buy regardless of current CPI. June 16 meeting is critical.

### AUD (5/9 — Mild Bull)
The RBA remains the most hawkish major central bank in the basket — third consecutive hike to 4.35% in May, fully unwinding 2025 easing. CPI 4.6% is well above the 2-3% band. Unemployment at 4.3% is within target. GDP growth annualised at 3.2% (Dec 2025 data). Westpac forecasts two more hikes to 4.85%; markets pricing at least one more. Drag: trade moved to deficit (-A$1.8B), PMIs only marginally expansionary. AUD is a carry currency supported by active tightening.

### USD (5/9 — Mild Bull)
Fed holding at 3.75% with no cuts in 2026 (JPM, BofA consensus). CPI 3.8% — well above 2% target and trending up (energy driven). Mfg PMI 54.5 strong. New: retail sales April came in at +0.5% MoM. Drags: GDP 2.0% QoQ (at threshold), Services PMI 51.0 (marginal), trade deficit -$60.3B. New Fed Chair (Powell term expired May 15) adds policy uncertainty but does not change underlying data.

### CAD (4/9 — Neutral)
Small improvements but still a sell-leaning currency. April CPI came in at 2.8% (up from 2.4%) — above target but BoC is not hiking. March retail +0.6% is a positive. But unemployment at 6.9% remains the worst in the G8 basket, GDP was -0.2% in Q4, and BoC is on hold. Most forecasters see no rate change through 2026. The combination of weak employment and negative growth is not conducive to CAD strength.

### CHF (3/9 — Mild Bear)
SNB at 0% (floor) with no hike until H2 2027. CPI 0.6% is well within the 0-2% target — no pressure to hike. SNB prefers FX intervention over rate adjustments. Both PMIs are actually strong (54.5/54.8) and the trade surplus is healthy, but the rate story dominates: zero rates with an 18-month timeline to any hike makes CHF a structural headwind currency.

### NZD (3/9 — Mild Bear)
RBNZ held in April at 2.25% but the situation is evolving. The committee's own forecast shows inflation jumping to 4.2% in the June quarter (above the 3% upper bound) — this is starting to build hike risk. May 27 RBNZ decision is the key watch event. PMIs are weak (50.5/48.9), GDP growth tepid at 0.2% QoQ. If the RBNZ signals a hike at the May 27 meeting, NZD shorts should be reassessed.

### PLN (2/9 — Strong Bear)
NBP cut in March (first cut of cycle, to 3.75%), held in April and May. Market FRAs price further cuts to ~3.5% by year-end. CPI at 3.2% is within the 1.5-3.5% band but not creating urgency to hold. Manufacturing PMI 48.8 is in contraction. Trade deficit -€497M. GDP at 0.5% QoQ is the only positive. NBP cited geopolitical uncertainty as reason to pause, but the cutting bias is clear. EUR/PLN is the preferred trade vehicle.

### NOK (4/9 — Neutral, ⚠️ Data-limited)
Norges Bank surprised with a 25bp hike to 4.25% in May — the first hike after signalling cuts earlier in 2026. CPI at 3.4% drove the decision. Large oil trade surplus supports NOK fundamentally. However, GDP contracted in Q4 (-0.3%), and critically Norway's Manufacturing PMI series (DNB/NIMA) was permanently discontinued in December 2025 — this data gap will persist for all future NOK analyses.

### SEK (4/9 — Treat as bearish despite score)
Sweden is the most contradictory data set in the basket. CPIF at -0.1% is deflation. Unemployment 9.7% is elevated. GDP contracted -0.2% QoQ. Riksbank has held at 1.75% for five consecutive meetings with rate path flat — easing risk if macro deteriorates. The high PMI (57.2) and retail (+3.1%) scores are anomalous and may reflect base effects. For FX purposes, deflation + cut risk dominates. Treat SEK as a weak currency.
