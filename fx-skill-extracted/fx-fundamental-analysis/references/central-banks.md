# Central Banks Reference — FX Fundamental Analysis

## G8 Currency Central Bank Details (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, AUD, NZD, CAD, CHF)

### USD — Federal Reserve (Fed)
- **Website**: federalreserve.gov
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (PCE, symmetric)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year (FOMC)
- **Key publications**: FOMC statement, Minutes (3 weeks after), SEP (quarterly)
- **Rate tool**: Federal Funds Rate (target range)
- **Hawkish signals**: Higher-for-longer language, upward dot plot revisions
- **Dovish signals**: "data dependent" softening, lower dot plot, mention of labour market deterioration

### EUR — European Central Bank (ECB)
- **Website**: ecb.europa.eu
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (HICP, medium-term)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year
- **Key publications**: Policy statement, press conference, Economic Bulletin
- **Rate tool**: Main Refinancing Rate, Deposit Facility Rate
- **Note**: Watch individual country PMIs (Germany, France, Italy) — divergence matters

### GBP — Bank of England (BoE)
- **Website**: bankofengland.co.uk
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (CPI)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year (MPC)
- **Key publications**: MPC minutes, Monetary Policy Report (quarterly)
- **Rate tool**: Bank Rate
- **Note**: MPC vote split often market-moving (e.g. 5-4 vs 7-2 = very different signals)

### JPY — Bank of Japan (BoJ)
- **Website**: boj.or.jp
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (CPI, sustained)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year
- **Key publications**: Policy statement, Outlook Report
- **Rate tool**: Short-term policy rate (moved from YCC)
- **Note**: BoJ historically most dovish G10 central bank; watch for hawkish pivots carefully
- **Note**: JPY weakness = carry trade dynamics — risk sentiment matters

### AUD — Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA)
- **Website**: rba.gov.au
- **Inflation target**: 2.0–3.0% band (medium-term)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year (changed from 11x in 2024)
- **Key publications**: Statement on Monetary Policy (quarterly), post-meeting statement
- **Rate tool**: Cash Rate Target
- **Note**: Commodity-linked (iron ore, coal) — China demand is a key driver

### NZD — Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ)
- **Website**: rbnz.govt.nz
- **Inflation target**: 1.0–3.0% (midpoint 2.0%)
- **Meeting frequency**: 7x per year
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Statement (quarterly), OCR review
- **Rate tool**: Official Cash Rate (OCR)
- **Note**: Small, open economy — often leads the cycle (was first to hike, first to cut post-COVID)

### CAD — Bank of Canada (BoC)
- **Website**: bankofcanada.ca
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (CPI, 1–3% control range)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Report (quarterly), rate statement
- **Rate tool**: Overnight Rate Target
- **Note**: Oil-linked — WTI/Brent prices correlate with CAD strength

### CHF — Swiss National Bank (SNB)
- **Website**: snb.ch
- **Inflation target**: 0–2% (price stability)
- **Meeting frequency**: 4x per year (quarterly)
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Assessment
- **Rate tool**: SNB Policy Rate
- **Note**: SNB has history of FX intervention — CHF is a safe-haven currency; watch EUR/CHF floor dynamics

## Extended Universe — Central Bank Details (SEK, NOK, SGD, PLN, HUF, DKK, MXN, INR, CNY, BRL, ILS)

### SEK — Riksbank (Sweden)
- **Website**: riksbank.se
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (CPIF — CPI with fixed mortgage interest rate)
- **Meeting frequency**: 6x per year
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Report (quarterly), rate decision statement
- **Rate tool**: Repo rate
- **Note**: Sweden is a small open economy; EUR/SEK closely tracks risk sentiment and ECB policy. Was in aggressive hiking cycle 2022–2023, cutting since 2024.

### NOK — Norges Bank (Norway)
- **Website**: norges-bank.no
- **Inflation target**: 2.0% (CPI-ATE, core; secondary CPI total)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Report (quarterly), rate decision
- **Rate tool**: Key policy rate (Styringsrenten)
- **Note**: NOK is heavily oil-linked — Brent crude price is the dominant driver. Watch Norges Bank Oil Fund flows. Thin liquidity outside EUR/NOK and USD/NOK.

### SGD — Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS)
- **Website**: mas.gov.sg
- **Inflation target**: ~2% implicit; primary tool is exchange rate not rate
- **Meeting frequency**: 2x per year (April and October)
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Statement, semi-annual
- **Rate tool**: S$NEER slope, centre-point, and width (not a policy rate)
- **Hawkish signals**: Steeper S$NEER slope, upward re-centring
- **Dovish signals**: Flatter slope, zero appreciation, or widened band
- **Note**: SGD is unique — MAS conducts monetary policy via exchange rate management. Standard rate bias scoring does not apply; see EM notes in SKILL.md.

### PLN — National Bank of Poland (NBP)
- **Website**: nbp.pl
- **Inflation target**: 2.5% (±1% = 1.5–3.5% tolerance band)
- **Meeting frequency**: 10x per year (monthly, with August and December typically off-cycle)
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Council statement, Inflation Report (quarterly)
- **Rate tool**: Reference rate
- **Note**: PLN is sensitive to EUR/PLN dynamics and political risk (EU rule-of-law tensions). In an active cutting cycle through 2024–2025.

### HUF — Magyar Nemzeti Bank / National Bank of Hungary (MNB)
- **Website**: mnb.hu
- **Inflation target**: 3.0% (±1% = 2–4% tolerance band)
- **Meeting frequency**: 12x per year (monthly Monetary Council)
- **Key publications**: Rate decision, Inflation Report (quarterly)
- **Rate tool**: Base rate
- **Note**: HUF is one of the most volatile EM European currencies. Sensitive to ECB policy, EU fund disbursements, and domestic political risk. Was in aggressive easing cycle from peak ~13% in 2023.

### DKK — Danmarks Nationalbank (DNB)
- **Website**: nationalbanken.dk
- **Inflation target**: None independent — mirrors ECB via ERM II peg
- **Meeting frequency**: Ad hoc (follows ECB)
- **Key publications**: Rate decision (typically follows ECB within hours)
- **Rate tool**: Certificates of deposit rate (tracks ECB deposit rate)
- **Note**: DKK is pegged to EUR within ±2.25% ERM II band. The Nationalbank's only goal is to defend the peg. Analyse DKK as EUR — independent DKK fundamental analysis is not meaningful.

### MXN — Banco de Mexico (Banxico)
- **Website**: banxico.org.mx
- **Inflation target**: 3.0% (±1% = 2–4% variability range)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year
- **Key publications**: Rate decision statement, quarterly Inflation Report
- **Rate tool**: Overnight interbank interest rate (Tasa de Interés Interbancaria a un día)
- **Note**: MXN is highly sensitive to USD/Mexico trade relations and US economic data (nearshoring flows). Carry trade currency — high rates attract flows when risk is on. Watch US tariff news carefully.

### INR — Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
- **Website**: rbi.org.in
- **Inflation target**: 4.0% (±2% = 2–6% tolerance band)
- **Meeting frequency**: 6x per year (bi-monthly Monetary Policy Committee)
- **Key publications**: MPC statement, Monetary Policy Report
- **Rate tool**: Repo rate
- **Note**: INR is partially managed — RBI intervenes heavily in USD/INR. Fundamental scoring is useful directionally but RBI interventions can cap moves. Watch for RBI FX reserve data as a signal of intervention intensity.

### CNY — People's Bank of China (PBOC)
- **Website**: pbc.gov.cn
- **Inflation target**: ~3% (informal annual government target; PBOC does not publish explicit CPI target)
- **Meeting frequency**: Monthly (PBOC Standing Committee), but rate changes announced ad hoc
- **Key publications**: Monetary Policy Implementation Report (quarterly), PBOC statements
- **Rate tool**: 7-day reverse repo rate (primary), 1-year MLF rate, LPR (loan prime rate)
- **Note**: CNY is not freely convertible. PBOC sets a daily fixing (midpoint) with ±2% intraday band. Rate decisions are opaque and politically influenced. Use CNH (offshore yuan) for market pricing. Track PBOC daily fixings vs market expectations for policy signals.

### BRL — Banco Central do Brasil (BCB)
- **Website**: bcb.gov.br
- **Inflation target**: 3.0% (±1.5% = 1.5–4.5%); target was 3.25% until end-2024, now 3.0%
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year (COPOM — Comitê de Política Monetária)
- **Key publications**: COPOM meeting minutes, Quarterly Inflation Report
- **Rate tool**: Selic rate (overnight target rate)
- **Note**: BRL is the highest-yielding major EM currency and a popular carry trade vehicle. Extremely sensitive to fiscal policy, Lula government spending, and commodity prices (soy, iron ore, oil). Political risk is a persistent discount. Very volatile — wide bid-ask spreads.

### ILS — Bank of Israel (BoI)
- **Website**: boi.org.il
- **Inflation target**: 1.0–3.0% (midpoint 2.0%)
- **Meeting frequency**: 8x per year (every 6 weeks, Monetary Committee)
- **Key publications**: Rate decision statement, biannual Inflation Report
- **Rate tool**: Bank of Israel rate
- **Note**: ILS is sensitive to geopolitical risk (Middle East tensions can trigger sharp ILS selloffs). BoI intervenes in FX markets to limit excessive volatility. Watch USD/ILS as the primary pair; most liquid.

## Central Bank Hawkish / Dovish Language Glossary

| Language | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| "Inflation remains persistent" | Hawkish — rates staying higher |
| "Data dependent" (new emphasis) | Could go either way — watch next data |
| "Restrictive for longer" | Hawkish |
| "Easing cycle has begun" | Dovish |
| "Gradual removal of accommodation" | Mildly hawkish |
| "Balanced risks to outlook" | Neutral pivot signal |
| "Labour market shows signs of cooling" | Dovish lean |
| "Prepared to act if necessary" | Conditional hawkish threat |
