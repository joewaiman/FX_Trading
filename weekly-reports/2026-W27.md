## 1. Economic Highlights

**USD — United States Dollar**
The dominant event of the week was a **sharply weaker-than-expected June Non-Farm Payrolls** print released Friday 3 July. Only **+57,000** jobs were added — versus the +110,000 consensus — while April and May were simultaneously revised down by a combined **74,000 jobs** (April: 179K→148K; May: 172K→129K). The unemployment rate ticked down to **4.2%** from 4.3%, cushioning the headline miss slightly. Leisure and hospitality shed 61,000 jobs, while health care added a modest 22,000. The miss dramatically pared expectations for the October Fed hike that markets had been pricing since the hawkish June 17 FOMC. Earlier in the week, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh attended the **ECB Sintra Forum** (June 29–July 2) alongside Lagarde, Bailey, and Macklem. Warsh reiterated that "prices are too high," declined to hint at the July 29 decision, and joined the other governors in a collective rejection of forward guidance. The US dollar index (DXY) retreated from its W26 peak near **101.80** to close the week around **100.0–100.2**.
