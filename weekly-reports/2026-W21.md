## 1. Economic Highlights

### USD — United States Dollar
The dominant story of the week was the Federal Reserve leadership transition: Jerome Powell's term officially concluded on May 15, and markets spent the opening days of W21 digesting the new Chair's first public communications. Initial USD selling on appointment uncertainty gave way to a recovery after the new Chair reiterated full data-dependence and maintained the Fed's hawkish narrative. April retail sales (+0.5% MoM, released W20) continued to underpin USD sentiment. CPI holds at **3.8% YoY** — well above target — with JPMorgan and BofA still forecasting no cuts in 2026 and a first hike in Q3 2027. Manufacturing PMI 54.5 remains strong; the persistent trade deficit (-$60.3B) is the primary drag. The DXY ended the week modestly lower, closing near 102.5 after W20's broad rally.

### EUR — Euro
EUR staged a modest technical recovery after last week's sharp selloff, closing at **1.1580**. The ECB June 5 meeting remains the dominant near-term catalyst: markets still price an **78% probability of a 25 bp hike** from 2.15% to 2.40%, driven by April CPI holding at 3.0% YoY — the highest since September 2023. Germany ifo Business Climate for May (released this week) ticked up marginally, offering a faint positive. However, Services PMI 47.6 (contraction), retail -0.1% MoM, and unemployment above 6.2% mean the ECB faces a classic stagflation dilemma — hiking to contain energy-driven inflation into a weakening services sector. EUR is a mild buy on rate expectations, not on economic health.

### GBP — British Pound
GBP was the **week's top performer**, rebounding +1.1% after W20's sharp correction from overbought levels (W20 RSI reached 37.22). The Q1 2026 GDP Flash (released May 15) confirmed **+0.6% QoQ (~2.4% annualised)** — the strongest quarterly growth in the G8 basket. CPI eased from 3.3% to **2.8% YoY** (April data), still 80bp above the 2% target — keeping a June hike on the table. The BoE voted 8-1 to hold at **3.75%** in April; the June 18 MPC meeting remains a live catalyst. Retail sales +0.7% MoM and both PMIs in expansion (Mfg 53.7, Svcs 52.7) underpin the bull case. GBP remains the highest-scored currency in the full 19-basket at 7/9.
