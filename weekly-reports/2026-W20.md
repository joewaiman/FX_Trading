## 1. Economic Highlights

### USD — United States Dollar

April Retail Sales rebounded sharply to **+0.5% MoM** (vs +0.2% forecast; −0.4% prior in March), confirming consumer resilience despite elevated prices. Initial Jobless Claims for the week ending May 17 came in at **238K** (vs 240K forecast; 235K prior), maintaining tight labour market conditions. April Industrial Production also beat expectations at **+0.3% MoM** (vs +0.1% forecast; −0.5% prior). Markets now price zero probability of any 2026 Fed rate cut, with hike risk edging back onto the table for 2027 — the 8-4 FOMC dissent split from April's meeting remains a backdrop warning.

### EUR — Euro
Eurozone Q1 2026 Flash GDP printed **+0.2% QoQ** (+0.8% annualized) — below the +0.4% QoQ forecast and essentially unchanged from Q4 2025's already-sluggish pace, confirming that ECB rate hike expectations are being built on a structurally weak economic base. Germany CPI Final April was confirmed at **+2.2% YoY** (in line with flash estimate) and France CPI Final at **+1.8% YoY** (in line). The EUR Trade Balance for March narrowed to **€13.8B** (from €15.4B prior), reflecting rising energy import costs. The stagflation narrative intensified this week: the ECB faces pressure to hike in June while services PMI sits at 47.6 (contraction), retail sales are negative, and GDP growth is near stagnation. EUR was the weakest G7 currency of the week.

### GBP — British Pound
The UK Q1 2026 Flash GDP beat expectations at **+0.6% QoQ** (vs +0.5% forecast; +0.4% prior in Q4 2025) — the fastest quarterly growth since Q2 2025 and confirmation that the UK economy is outperforming the Eurozone materially. GDP growth on a YoY basis stood at **+1.1%**. Strong services output and resilient household spending drove the beat. GBP was the best-performing major G10 currency (ex-JPY) for the week. The next Bank of England MPC meeting is June 18; markets continue to price at least one 25bp hike by H2 2026 given CPI at 3.3% vs the 2% target.

### JPY — Japanese Yen
Japan's Q1 2026 Flash GDP printed **+1.3% annualized** (vs −1.2% forecast and +0.8% prior in Q4 2025) — a remarkable reversal that shocked consensus, driven by a surge in private consumption and strong export revenues. This is the single most important data surprise of W20, and it dramatically reinforced the Bank of Japan's case for a rate hike as early as June or July 2026. Bloomberg confirmed on May 12 that the BoJ is actively signaling a hike: FY2026 core CPI is forecast at 2.8% (up from 1.9%), and spring wage negotiations produced average increases above 5% for the third consecutive year. JPY was the week's dominant outperformer, surging **+2.50% vs USD** (USD/JPY: 148.50 → 144.88). The Nikkei 225 surged to a new all-time high on the GDP surprise.

AUD remained broadly supported by the active rate cycle despite the trade deficit and marginal PMI readings. Markets moved from pricing 25% probability of a hike to approximately 45% probability following the survey release. NZD outperformed on the expectation shift, gaining +0.44% vs USD. The May 27 RBNZ decision is the dominant upcoming catalyst for NZD.

### CNY — Chinese Yuan Renminbi

The PBOC maintained its USD/CNY fixing in the 6.80–6.83 range with a mild easing bias.
